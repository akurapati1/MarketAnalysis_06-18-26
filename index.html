Geopolitical & macro impact

US–Iran ceasefire & Strait of Hormuz reopening — oil keeps sliding

The dominant cross-asset driver remains the framework deal between Washington and Tehran: a 60-day ceasefire plus a reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, which had been effectively blockaded since the war began in late February. Crude has retraced from a wartime high near $120 to roughly $75–76 (WTI) and $79–80 (Brent), easing inflation worries and lifting risk appetite earlier in the week. But the deal is fragile — speaking at the G7 on Wednesday, President Trump called it "not final" and warned the US could "go right back to dropping bombs," while analysts note it could take two to three months to clear the ~500 stranded vessels and normalise flows. That leaves a two-way tape: every step toward implementation pressures oil further; any breakdown sends it spiking back.
